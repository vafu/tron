# IR leverage and constraints

Timestamp: 2026-05-02T13:22:25-07:00

Tags: #tron

Question:

How should Tron leverage IR for a 3D/2D pointer system given the practical constraints of Windows Hello-style camera modules?

Current use:

IR is used as a helper channel. The current pipeline subtracts an IR background, estimates an IR foreground mask, and uses that mask to darken non-hand RGB pixels. This improves MediaPipe hand tracking stability because the RGB image presented to the landmarker is less cluttered.

Known constraints:

- IR is reliable mainly indoors, and outdoors only in low-light/night conditions. Daytime sunlight can saturate or swamp the signal.
- Non-directional IR emission is weak for absolute distance estimation because brightness varies with surface reflectance, angle, exposure, and emitter behavior.
- The IR flashlight appears to strobe. With a 30 fps IR stream, usable lit frames may effectively be 15 fps, which is poor for responsive hand tracking.
- Windows Hello camera stacks may hide or lock exposure/emitter controls behind proprietary UVC extension units or firmware behavior.

Working position:

Accept IR as a constrained but valuable signal, not as the sole tracking source. IR should help segmentation, reacquisition, confidence estimation, and possibly relative depth, while RGB landmarks and temporal filtering remain primary for smooth pointer motion.

Depth hypotheses:

- Landmark scale can estimate relative `z` when the hand pose is stable.
- IR foreground area/intensity can contribute to relative `z`, but only after exposure/emitter state is characterized.
- Proximity sensor data may provide an independent near-field cue.
- Fusion is likely better than a pure IR depth estimate.

Open risk:

If exposure cannot be controlled or measured, IR intensity may be too unstable for distance. In that case, the project should treat IR as segmentation/reacquisition only and train/customize a model around RGB landmarks plus coarse proximity.
