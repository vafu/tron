# Detailed stereo calibration plan

Timestamp: 2026-05-07T21:04:30-07:00

Question / hypothesis: How should tron solve RGB/IR calibration when cameras have physical separation and different FOV/resolution?

Observed result: The two problems must be solved separately and then combined through stereo geometry. Physical camera separation is solved by stereo extrinsics: rotation R and translation T from the IR camera coordinate system into the RGB camera coordinate system. Translation T is the camera baseline. Different FOV, resolution, optical center, and lens distortion are solved by per-camera intrinsics: RGB fx/fy/cx/cy/distortion and IR fx/fy/cx/cy/distortion.

The current affine calibration is only rgb_x = ir_x * scale_x + offset_x and rgb_y = ir_y * scale_y + offset_y. That can roughly compensate FOV/size mismatch and center offset at one depth, but it cannot model parallax. The correct mapping between an IR pixel and an RGB pixel changes as the target moves closer or farther from the cameras. Therefore affine calibration can look acceptable for the checkerboard plane where it was captured and then drift for hands at other depths.

A proper stereo profile should contain: RGB camera matrix and distortion coefficients, IR camera matrix and distortion coefficients, IR-to-RGB rotation R, IR-to-RGB translation T, optional essential/fundamental matrices, rectification transforms/maps, image sizes/modes used during calibration, and quality metrics such as RMS and per-view errors.

Runtime should expose geometry operations instead of one global 2D warp: IR pixel to RGB epipolar line, triangulate matched RGB/IR points into 3D, project an IR ray into RGB with an assumed depth, rectify RGB/IR frames or points, and optionally convert between normalized image coordinates and camera rays.

Important caveat: after stereo calibration, an arbitrary IR pixel still does not map to one exact RGB pixel unless depth is known. It maps to an epipolar line in RGB. To get a point, tron needs either a matching feature in both cameras, an assumed depth plane, or a depth estimate from another cue such as transient IR brightness, landmark scale, hand-model priors, or proximity.

Follow-up tasks / instrumentation gaps: Rework tron-calib as a quality-gated sample collector. Capture 20-40 good paired samples instead of 3. Show coverage for X/Y position, board size/depth, and skew/tilt. Reject near-duplicate poses. Prefer findChessboardCornersSB over the older checkerboard detector. Use OpenCV calibrate_camera_extended and stereo_calibrate_extended so per-view reprojection errors are available. Auto-drop bad samples and re-solve. Save only when RMS/epipolar quality passes a threshold. For hand tracking, combine calibrated RGB/IR rays with landmark or ROI correspondences plus IR brightness, landmark scale, or proximity depth priors to produce a more stable 3D pointer estimate.
