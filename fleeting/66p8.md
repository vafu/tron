# MJPG decode should target BGRA for wgpu

Timestamp: 2026-05-07T23:15:01-07:00

Question / hypothesis: Is BGRA a better decoded RGB texture target than RGBA for MJPG camera frames?

Observed result: tron-stream MJPG path was changed from OpenCV BGR decode plus BGR2RGBA into BGR decode plus BGR2BGRA, uploaded as wgpu TextureFormat::Bgra8Unorm. Lenovo RGB at 1280x720 MJPG 30 fps still negotiated interval 1/30 and delivered 30 fps. Decode plus conversion measured about 4.1-4.5 ms/frame, publish about 0.27-0.30 ms, upload about 0.11-0.13 ms, render about 0.08-0.10 ms. Frame age was low in this run, around 5-6 ms.

Interpretation: BGRA is the correct current target for OpenCV MJPG decode into wgpu. It avoids the extra channel reorder needed for RGBA and matches a native wgpu texture format. This is still an alpha-adding conversion, but it is cheaper and simpler than BGR-to-RGBA.

Follow-up tasks: Promote BGRA as the main RGB decoded frame format when adding MJPG support to the shared camera backend. Later compare with libjpeg-turbo direct BGRA decode to remove the OpenCV cvtColor pass.
